\chapter{Results}
\label{ch:results}

The final evaluation used the 1437-complex analysis subset for which paired full and interface-restricted graph views were available. The comparison contained 52 models: 48 GraphVAE variants and 4 direct supervised baselines. Every reported metric is the mean over 10 outer held-out fits from 5 folds and 2 repeats. The complete 52-model table is given in Appendix~\ref{ch:appendix_matrix}.

\section{Headline models}

Table~\ref{tab:headline-models} lists the strongest models in the comparison. The best overall model was the direct supervised baseline on the full static graph. The best GraphVAE was the full static semi-supervised model with latent dimension 32. The best interface model was the static supervised baseline.

\section{Factor comparisons}

Table~\ref{tab:factor-summary} summarises the main matched comparisons in the GraphVAE set. The dominant effect was graph view: the full graph outperformed the interface-restricted graph in all 24 matched GraphVAE comparisons. Under the static reconstruction target (\texttt{shared\_static}), the static representation outperformed the static-plus-dynamic representation in 11 of 12 matched GraphVAE comparisons. Semi-supervision helped consistently for the full-graph runs and the interface static runs, but not for the interface dynamic runs.

Within the full semi-supervised $SD$ family, the reconstruction target also mattered. Averaged over latent dimensions 8, 16, and 32, the mean test RMSE was 2.4434 for \texttt{shared\_static}, 2.4795 for \texttt{sd\_static\_plus\_dynamic\_all}, and 2.5150 for \texttt{sd\_dynamic\_all}. The corresponding mean test Pearson correlations were 0.4682, 0.4335, and 0.4221.

\section{Performance by interaction class}

The matched 1437-complex analysis set contained 966 complexes in the broad \emph{other} class, 366 antibody--antigen complexes, and 105 TCR-pMHC complexes. For subgroup analysis, the outer-test predictions were pooled over all 10 fits, so each complex contributes two held-out predictions, one from each repeat.

\section{Summary}

Four results dominate the comparison. First, the full graph was better than the interface-restricted representation. Second, the dynamic summaries did not improve held-out performance over the static representation. Third, the strongest model was the direct supervised baseline on the full static graph. Fourth, the same model ordering remained in the antibody--antigen, TCR-pMHC, and other interaction classes.
